Why is there a different result?

There are 4 types of Clean Air Zone that charge different types of vehicle. For example, Birmingham charges private cars (CAZ D) but Leeds does not (CAZ B).
We calculate the charge on whether your vehicle meets the minimum emission standards and if its type will be charged in that zone.

You do not need to pay until summer 2020 when the zones are live.

The zone will operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, every day of the year.

Exemptions, refunds and alternatives to driving

Contact the local authority of the Clean Air Zone you want to drive in for more information on:

  • exemptions
  • refunds
  • support to upgrade or replace your vehicle
  • cycling and public transport options

Important information about vehicle data

  • The data accuracy of this service is always improving. It is recommended you recheck your vehicle shortly before you intend to drive in any Clean Air Zones.
